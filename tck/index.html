What is a TCK (Third Culture Kid)?


Third culture kids (TCK) are individuals who are (or were as children) raised in a culture other than their parents' or their culture of their country of nationality, and live in such an environment during a significant part of their early development years.

TCKs move between cultures before they have had the opportunity to fully develop their personal and cultural identity.

What are some characteristics of TCKs?

"Where are you from?" can be a dreaded question to answer.
Let's be real - when you've lived in a bunch of different places, it can be tiring to explain.

You've lived in more than 2 cities across different countries.
You're familiar with impermanence. One day you find yourself moving to a city across the world, whether you want to or not.

You own multiple passports.
There are perks to having more than one citizenship.

You don't live in the country you were born in.
It's always a strange thing when you go back to your home country... only to experience a culture shock.
